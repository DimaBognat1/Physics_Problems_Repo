# Problem 3 — Elimination of time and interpretation of acceleration

The parametric equations of the motion are:

x(t) = 2t²  
y(t) = 3t³

---

## 1. Eliminate the parameter t

From the first equation:

t² = x / 2

t = √(x/2)

Substitute this into the equation for y:

y = 3t³

y = 3(√(x/2))³

y = 3(x/2)^(3/2)

This is the equation of the trajectory.

---

## 2. Trajectory

The trajectory is described by the equation:

y = 3(x/2)^(3/2)

This curve is not a straight line or a parabola.  
It is a nonlinear trajectory determined by the relation between x and y.

---

## 3. Velocity

Velocity components are the derivatives of position:

vx(t) = dx/dt = 4t

vy(t) = dy/dt = 9t²

Velocity vector:

v(t) = (4t, 9t²)

Magnitude of velocity:

|v(t)| = √((4t)² + (9t²)²)

|v(t)| = √(16t² + 81t⁴)

---

## 4. Acceleration

Acceleration components are derivatives of velocity:

ax(t) = dvx/dt = 4

ay(t) = dvy/dt = 18t

Acceleration vector:

a(t) = (4, 18t)

Magnitude of acceleration:

|a(t)| = √(4² + (18t)²)

|a(t)| = √(16 + 324t²)

---

## 5. Is acceleration constant?

No.

The x-component of acceleration is constant:

ax = 4

But the y-component depends on time:

ay = 18t

Therefore the acceleration vector changes with time.
