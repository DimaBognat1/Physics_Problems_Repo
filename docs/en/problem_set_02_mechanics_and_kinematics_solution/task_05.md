# Problem 5 — Elliptical motion (purely kinematic)

The parametric equations of the motion are:

x(t) = a cos(ωt)  
y(t) = b sin(ωt)

---

## 1. Velocity

Velocity components are the derivatives of the position:

vx(t) = dx/dt = -aω sin(ωt)

vy(t) = dy/dt = bω cos(ωt)

Velocity vector:

v(t) = (-aω sin(ωt), bω cos(ωt))

Magnitude of velocity:

|v(t)| = √[(aω sin(ωt))² + (bω cos(ωt))²]

|v(t)| = ω √[a² sin²(ωt) + b² cos²(ωt)]

---

## 2. Acceleration

Acceleration components are derivatives of velocity:

ax(t) = dvx/dt = -aω² cos(ωt)

ay(t) = dvy/dt = -bω² sin(ωt)

Acceleration vector:

a(t) = (-aω² cos(ωt), -bω² sin(ωt))

Magnitude of acceleration:

|a(t)| = √[(aω² cos(ωt))² + (bω² sin(ωt))²]

|a(t)| = ω² √[a² cos²(ωt) + b² sin²(ωt)]

---

## 3. Is the velocity magnitude constant?

No.

The magnitude of velocity depends on time because the terms sin(ωt) and cos(ωt) change with time.

Therefore the speed varies along the elliptical trajectory.

---

## 4. Where is the velocity maximum?

The velocity is maximal where the expression

a² sin²(ωt) + b² cos²(ωt)

is maximal.

This depends on the values of a and b.  
The maximum speed occurs near the ends of the shorter axis of the ellipse.

---

## 5. Trajectory

From the parametric equations we obtain the equation of an ellipse:

x²/a² + y²/b² = 1

Thus the body moves along an elliptical trajectory.
