# Problem 2 — Projectile motion

A body moves in the Earth's gravitational field without air resistance.  
The initial velocity is \(v_0\) and the launch angle relative to the horizontal is \(\alpha\).

---

## 1. Equations of motion

We decompose the initial velocity into horizontal and vertical components:

\[
v_{0x} = v_0 \cos(\alpha)
\]

\[
v_{0y} = v_0 \sin(\alpha)
\]

### Horizontal motion

Since there is no acceleration in the horizontal direction:

\[
x(t) = v_0 \cos(\alpha) \, t
\]

---

### Vertical motion

The motion in the vertical direction is affected by gravity:

\[
y(t) = v_0 \sin(\alpha) \, t - \frac{1}{2} g t^2
\]

where \(g\) is the gravitational acceleration.

---

## 2. Time of flight

The projectile lands when \(y(t) = 0\).

Solving for time:

\[
T = \frac{2 v_0 \sin(\alpha)}{g}
\]

---

## 3. Maximum height

The maximum height reached by the projectile is:

\[
H = \frac{v_0^2 \sin^2(\alpha)}{2g}
\]

---

## 4. Range

The horizontal range of the projectile is:

\[
R = \frac{v_0^2 \sin(2\alpha)}{g}
\]

---

## 5. Angle for maximum range

The range is maximal when:

\[
\alpha = 45^\circ
\]

---

## 6. Trajectory visualization

The trajectory of the projectile is a parabola.  
Changing the angle \(\alpha\) changes the height and the range of the motion.
