# Problem 1 — Uniform and uniformly accelerated motion

The equation of motion is given:

x(t) = x₀ + v₀t + ½at²

---

## 1. Velocity and acceleration

Velocity is the derivative of position with respect to time:

v(t) = dx/dt

Taking the derivative of x(t):

v(t) = v₀ + at

Acceleration is the derivative of velocity:

a(t) = dv/dt

Therefore:

a(t) = a

The acceleration is constant.

---

## 2. Given parameters

x₀ = 0  
v₀ = 5 m/s  
a = −2 m/s²

---

### Stopping time

The object stops when the velocity becomes zero:

v(t) = v₀ + at

5 − 2t = 0

t = 2.5 s

---

### Maximum velocity

Since the acceleration is negative, the velocity decreases with time.  
Therefore the maximum velocity occurs at the beginning:

v_max = v₀ = 5 m/s

---

### Maximum displacement

The position function becomes:

x(t) = 5t − t²

Substitute the stopping time t = 2.5 s:

x = 5(2.5) − (2.5)²  
x = 12.5 − 6.25  
x = 6.25 m

---

## 3. Interpretation

x(t) is a parabolic function.  
v(t) is a straight line decreasing with time.  
a(t) is constant and equal to −2 m/s².
