# Problem 8 — Relative motion

Body A moves with velocity:

\[
\vec{v}_A = (3, 1)
\]

Body B moves with velocity:

\[
\vec{v}_B = (1, -2)
\]

---

## 1. Relative velocity \( \vec{v}_{A/B} \)

The relative velocity of body A with respect to body B is:

\[
\vec{v}_{A/B} = \vec{v}_A - \vec{v}_B
\]

Substitute the values:

\[
\vec{v}_{A/B} = (3,1) - (1,-2)
\]

\[
\vec{v}_{A/B} = (2,3)
\]

---

## 2. Direction of the relative motion

The direction of the relative motion is determined by the vector:

\[
\vec{v}_{A/B} = (2,3)
\]

This means that, in the reference frame of body B, body A moves:

- to the right (positive x-direction),
- upward (positive y-direction).

The angle of motion relative to the x-axis is:

\[
\theta = \arctan\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)
\]

So the motion is directed above the x-axis.

---

## 3. Interpretation in different reference frames

### Reference frame attached to the origin

- Body A moves with velocity \((3,1)\)
- Body B moves with velocity \((1,-2)\)

### Reference frame attached to body A

- Body A is at rest
- Body B moves with velocity:

\[
\vec{v}_{B/A} = \vec{v}_B - \vec{v}_A = (-2,-3)
\]

### Reference frame attached to body B

- Body B is at rest
- Body A moves with velocity:

\[
\vec{v}_{A/B} = (2,3)
\]

---

## 4. Conclusion

The relative velocity of A with respect to B is:

\[
\vec{v}_{A/B} = (2,3)
\]

So body A moves relative to body B in the positive x and positive y directions.
