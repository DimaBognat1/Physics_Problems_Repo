# Problem 10 — Angular momentum in circular motion

The position vector is

r(t) = (R cos(ωt), R sin(ωt), 0)

---

# 1. Velocity

Velocity is the derivative of the position vector.

v(t) = dr/dt

v(t) = (−Rω sin(ωt), Rω cos(ωt), 0)

---

# 2. Angular momentum

Angular momentum is defined as

L = m (r × v)

Compute the cross product.

r = (R cos(ωt), R sin(ωt), 0)

v = (−Rω sin(ωt), Rω cos(ωt), 0)

r × v = (0, 0, R²ω)

Therefore

L = m (0, 0, R²ω)

---

# 3. Magnitude of angular momentum

|L| = mR²ω

Since m, R and ω are constants,

|L| is constant.

---

# 4. Direction of angular momentum

The vector L points in the positive z-direction, perpendicular to the plane of motion.

The direction follows the **right-hand rule**.

---

# Conclusion

For uniform circular motion:

• angular momentum is constant  
• its magnitude is mR²ω  
• the vector is perpendicular to the plane of motion
